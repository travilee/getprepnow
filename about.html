Travis Barnhart and the Good Samaritan Project

Hello, my name is Travis Barnhart. I'm a social worker and I currently serve as the Director of Prevention at Good Samaritan Project in Kansas City, Missouri.

Good Samaritan Project is Kansas City's longest-running HIV/AIDS service organization. Our agency started in 1985 in response to the AIDS crisis. Over our 35+ years of existence we've grown and changed with the shifts in the HIV/AIDS epidemic and today we provide a comprehensive set of services including HIV case management and HIV and STD (sexually transmitted diseases) testing and prevention.

I have been working in the HIV and sexual health field since 1999 when I first started volunteering and then working at a local AIDS Service Organization. I also worked at Planned Parenthood, a state health department, and the Kansas City CARE Clinic prior to joining the staff at Good Samaritan Project in 2014. I currently lead the Kansas City PrEP Task Force through the Kansas City Health Department's Ryan White Planning Council.

You can learn more about me and the Good Samaritan Project (GSP) by visiting GSP's website.
